## Week-by-week milestones & suggested tasks

Week 3 (Mon Jan 26)
- Team formation and project selection in class.
- Deliverable: **Project choice & justification** (first and second picks) — prepare and submit by Week 4 Monday.
- Action: Assign roles; schedule weekly meeting time.

Week 4 (Mon Feb 2)
- Deliverable: Submit project picks (due Week 4 Monday).
- Primary analysis planning: data inventory, file formats, initial QC plan.
- Lab report: project selection + plan.

Week 5 (Mon Feb 9) — Week 6 (Mon Feb 16)
- Primary analysis: raw data QC, trimming, alignment/assembly, basic summary stats.
- Deliverable: primary-analysis results (intermediate figures, commands used).
- Lab reports: progress + commands + problems.

Week 7 (Mon Feb 23) — Week 9 (Mon Mar 9)
- Secondary analysis: differential expression / variant calling / assembly polishing / annotation depending on project.
- Deliverable: secondary-analysis results and parameter justifications.

Week 10 (Mon Mar 16) — Week 12 (Mon Mar 30)
- Tertiary analysis or validation: functional enrichment, validation on external data, cross-checks, visualization.
- If pursuing bonus work, obtain instructor approval now.
- Deliverable: tertiary-analysis results or preliminary bonus-analysis plan.

Week 13 (Mon Apr 6) — Week 14 (Mon Apr 13)
- Draft final paper (Draft 1 & 2): assemble Introduction, Methods, Results (primary + secondary), Discussion items.
- Deliverable: Draft 1 (Week 13), Draft 2 (Week 14)

Week 15 (Mon Apr 20)
- Final polishing, create figures, finalize methods and references.
- Deliverable: Draft 3 and near-final paper; prepare presentation if required.

Week 16 (Mon Apr 27)
- Final paper due **Mon Apr 27, 11:59pm** (submit to myCourses dropbox; no late submissions).
- Deliverable: Final paper (group) + any required peer evaluations.

## Roles & meeting template
- Roles (choose and rotate if desired):
  - Coordinator (schedules meetings, tracks deadlines)
  - Data lead (runs QC, preprocessing)
  - Methods lead (implements analyses & pipelines)
  - Documentation lead (maintains lab notes & writes reports)
  - Presentation lead (figures and final slide deck)

- Weekly meeting agenda (30–60 min):
  1. Quick status updates (5–10 min)
  2. Review last week’s work & lab reports (10–15 min)
  3. Assign tasks & set goals for next week (10–20 min)
  4. Roadblocks / help needed (5–10 min)

Include these sections in each weekly report (3/4 to 2 pages for undergrads / 1–2 pages for grads):

- Problems encountered
- Goals for the coming week
